Project Checklist

Gather the requirements for the project & choose considerations based on the below-mentioned factors.

Purpose of the Checklist

This checklist won't give concrete answers for your project, because it depends majorly on the project requirements. Instead this checklist creates an awareness of all the different aspects that need to be considered for Azure solutions.

Physical Infrastructure​

Learn more about the Physical Infrastructure

Azure Region​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Have you considered the Azure region to minimize latency and improve performance for your end users?
Have you considered ensuring that the Azure region you select complies with data residency and regulatory requirements specific to your industry or customer needs?
Have you considered verifying that the Azure services or VM types you plan to use are available in the region you selected?
Have you considered the cost differences across regions and selected the one that aligns with your budgetary constraints?

Availability Zones​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Have you considered choosing Availability Zones to ensure your application is highly available and resilient to datacenter-level failures?
For your mission-critical workloads, did you consider using Availability Zones to provide an added layer of redundancy and reliability?
Is data protection against hardware failures or natural disasters a priority, prompting you to deploy resources across multiple Availability Zones?
Did you consider selecting Availability Zones within the same region to minimize latency during failover and ensure fast recovery times?

Region Pairs​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Did you consider choosing to use region pairs to ensure business continuity in the event of a major disaster, allowing services to failover to the other region if one is affected?
For redundancy across a large geographical area, have you considered selecting region pairs to mitigate risks associated with regional outages, such as natural disasters or widespread network failures?
Have you considered using region pairs to achieve high service availability with automatic failover, ensuring that your service remains accessible even if one region experiences an outage?

Management Infrastructure​

Learn more about the Management Infrastructure

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
When organizing your resources, did you consider billing, quota limits and security boundary requirements to ensure effective management and protection of your project?
Have you decided, whether to manage project environments (Dev, QA, Staging, Prod) at the subscription or resource group level, considering factors such as quota limits and security boundaries? Read more about subscription quota limits
Did you consider evaluating the nature of each project to determine if compliance or policy standards were needed and use Management Groups accordingly?
Have you considered determining the billing requirements of your project to decide whether to use a single subscription or multiple subscriptions based on the project's needs?
Have you consider devising a plan for RBAC management and applying the principle of least privilege to ensure appropriate resource access? Learn more about security best practices

Management Groups Scope​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Did you consider configuring Azure policies and role-based access control (RBAC) to ensure consistent compliance and effective resource management across your subscriptions?
Did you consider grouping your Azure subscriptions to simplify billing, reporting and cost management?

Subscriptions Scope​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Did you consider grouping resources under specific subscriptions to track and manage costs for individual projects?
Have you considered using different payment methods for separate subscriptions to manage costs by client or project?
Have you set up resource quotas and limits to control usage and manage costs within specific projects?
Have you consider creating separate subscriptions to establish distinct security boundaries for different projects or teams?
Have you considered to use separate subscriptions for different environments (Dev, QA, Staging, Prod) to manage resource quotas and ensure clear separation?

Resource Groups Scope​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Have you grouped related resources (e.g., VMs, databases, storage accounts) together based on their lifecycle or project requirements?
Have you managed and deployed resources collectively within a resource group, applying tags, access controls, or policies to all resources simultaneously?
Have you assigned role-based access control (RBAC) to your resource groups to control who can manage the resources within them?
Have you considered using separate resource groups for different environments (Dev, QA, Staging, Prod) to facilitate centralized billing and management under a single subscription?

Naming Convention​

Learn more about the Naming Convention

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Does your naming convention for Azure resources include a short abbreviation for the resource type to avoid confusion?
Have you ensured that all resource names are in lowercase and use hyphens to separate words for better readability?
Does your naming convention include an identifier for the environment (e.g., dev, qa, prod) and a region code (e.g., wus for West US) as recommended?

Azure Sub-Resource Naming​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Does the names of sub-resources, such as containers or subnets, appropriately follow a naming convention that omits environment or region details, as these are covered by the parent resource?
For sub-resources like subnets or secrets, have you used short, meaningful names that fit within any character set or length restrictions specific to the resource?
Have you avoided using hyphens in names for resources that have character set restrictions, such as storage accounts?

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason

Is the responsibility for managing identities in Microsoft Entra ID primarily handled by your organization's IT team?

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Have you synchronized your on-premises Active Directory with Microsoft Entra ID using Microsoft Entra ID Connect?
Have you considered having Entra Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) enabled to require users to provide a second form of authentication for added security?
Have you implemented Conditional Access policies to automate control decisions based on user conditions and access controls?
Have you considered using Microsoft Entra ID Privileged Identity Management (PIM) to manage, control and monitor access to critical resources in your organization?

Have you implemented disaster recovery plans and strategies to ensure business continuity and safeguard critical workloads in Azure?

Have you considered using Azure Policies to ensure consistent application of tags for cost management across your resources?
Have you considered configuring Azure Policies to require resources to send diagnostic logs to a Log Analytics workspace?
When implementing Azure Policies, have you considered factors such as regulatory compliance, resource standardization, cost control and security?

Have you leveraged Azure Blueprint to speed up the deployment process by automating the creation of resources and applying policies in a single step?

Have you considered applying Azure tags to help ensure compliance with organizational policies and enforce tagging standards across your resources?
Have you maintained consistency in tag values, especially for project environments like dev, staging and production, to ensure clarity and effective management?

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Before implementing Azure Management Groups, have you considered evaluating whether the project requirements and organizational structure justify their use?
Have you considered using Azure Management Groups to manage access, policies and compliance across multiple Azure subscriptions?
Have you considered applying governance policies consistently across all subscriptions within a management group to ensure uniform policy enforcement?
Have you considered using Azure Management Groups to assign role-based access control (RBAC) at a broader level than individual subscriptions?

Azure RBAC​

Learn more about the Azure RBAC

Azure RBAC Roles​

Learn more about the Azure RBAC

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Have you assigned built-in roles (e.g., Owner, Contributor, Reader) based on team members' responsibilities and the actions they need to perform?
Have you used the Azure portal to assign roles at the appropriate scope (subscription, resource group, or individual resource)?
Have you ensured that each team member has the minimum level of access required to perform their tasks, adhering to the principle of least privilege?
Have you created custom roles when built-in roles do not meet specific project needs?
Have you determined the Azure resources needed for the project and identified who needs access to these resources and what actions they need to perform (e.g., read, write, delete)?
Have you used Azure RBAC to enforce security policies by limiting access to sensitive resources and ensuring compliance with security requirements?

Principle of Least Privilege​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Have you implemented the Principle of Least Privilege by ensuring that users are granted only the minimum level of access necessary for their job functions?
Have you regularly reviewed and adjusted user permissions to ensure they continue to adhere to the Principle of Least Privilege?

Types of Azure Monitoring​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Have you considered using Infrastructure Monitoring to assess resource utilization and detect performance bottlenecks in virtual machines, networks and storage?
Is Application Monitoring part of your strategy for tracking performance metrics like response times, error rates and user interactions for your applications?
Have you implemented Network Monitoring to identify network latency, connectivity issues and analyze traffic patterns in your Virtual Networks and Load Balancers?
Are you utilizing Security Monitoring to detect potential security threats, identify vulnerabilities and enforce security best practices?

Azure VPN Gateway & Express Route​

Learn more about the Azure VPN Gateway & Express Route

Azure VPN Gateway​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Is cost a significant factor in your decision to use Azure VPN Gateway for connecting your on-premises network to Azure?
Have you determined whether you need to provide secure remote access to individual users or remote offices using Azure VPN Gateway?
Have you evaluated whether the performance of Azure VPN Gateway, which depends on internet traffic, meets your requirements?
Are you considering using Azure VPN Gateway for smaller workloads or limited bandwidth requirements?
Have you assessed the security of Azure VPN Gateway, which uses IPsec encryption over the public internet, to ensure it aligns with your security needs?
Have you assessed whether Azure VPN Gateway or Azure ExpressRoute is necessary for your project based on its specific requirements and connectivity needs?

Azure Express Route​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Have you identified high bandwidth requirements that necessitate using Azure ExpressRoute for transferring large amounts of data?
Is regulatory compliance a concern that requires your traffic to avoid traversing the public internet, making Azure ExpressRoute a suitable choice?
Have you identified a need for global connectivity with high reliability and performance to connect multiple global offices, which Azure ExpressRoute can provide?
Is Azure ExpressRoute a viable option for your deployment if you require a high-performance, dedicated connection between your on-premises infrastructure and Azure?
Have you considered the higher cost of Azure ExpressRoute and whether it fits your budget given the benefits of high performance, security and availability?

Secrets Management​

Learn more about the Secrets Management

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason
Have you considered identifying which resources and services require authentication to other Azure services or third-party systems and assessed the best methods for managing these authentications securely?
Have you implemented a secure process for storing, managing and accessing sensitive information such as API keys, passwords and certificates to ensure they are protected from unauthorized access while being available to authorized applications and services?
Have you transitioned from traditional methods of managing secrets, such as embedding tokens or passwords directly into application code or configuration files, to using Azure Key Vault for centralized and secure management of sensitive information?
Have you adopted Azure Managed Identity for managing authentication to Azure services, thereby eliminating the need to store credentials in your code and azure key vault enhancing security through automatic identity management?
Have you assessed whether Azure Managed Identity can meet all your authentication needs and decided between using System-assigned or User-assigned Managed Identity based on your specific requirements?

Have you applied Network Security Groups (NSGs) to control inbound and outbound traffic for each subnet?
Have you used separate subnets for different environments (e.g., development, staging, production) and applied tailored Network Security Groups (NSGs) to enforce specific security policies and access controls?

Have you considered assigning NSGs to subnets to apply security rules to all resources within the subnet?
Have you applied NSGs to individual network interfaces (NICs) to control traffic specifically for associated resources like virtual machines?

Have you considered utilizing DDoS Protection Standard to manage costs associated with unexpected traffic spikes and autoscaling during a DDoS attack?
Are you leveraging the basic DDoS Protection included with Azure services to protect against common network-layer attacks?
Have you enabled DDoS Protection for your Azure Virtual Network to enhance network security, while considering that it might not be necessary if you are already using Application Gateway or Azure Firewall?

Private Endpoints​

ConsiderationsApplicable (Yes/No)Reason

Depending on your project requirements, Have you selected between Service Endpoints, Private Endpoints, or a combination of both to securely connect your resources?
